About

We are setting up the state-of-the-art Ultrahigh Vacuum based Surface and Interface Science research group in IIT Bombay.

Our Interest

Group research interests are focused on (but not limited to) electronic structure studies of:

  • Quantum materials such as Quantum Spin Hall materials
  • Heterostructure of layered materials
  • Metallic and Oxide Quasicrystals
  • Organic and Inorganic adsorbates
  • Design and Fabrications of State of the Art Instruments

For more details feel free to contact over email or in-person.

Ongoing Projects

There are multiple choices of projects available immediately for short and long term. Proposed topics are:

  • In-situ growth & characterisation of graphene analog 2D materials
  • Growth & investigation of Oxide Quasicrystals -Structure studies using Low Energy Electron Diffraction
  • Inorganic and Organic adsorbates adsorption studies on Oxide Quasicrystals
  • Designing the Heterostructure by mechanical stacking and its surface structure studies
  • Developing electronics circuits suitable for piezoelectric transducer for nanometer scale spatial imaging
  • Designing and development of tools for photon counter using digital signal processing method
  • Design and implementation of spin-polarised and non-polarised electron source for surface science investigation

Feel free to contact for more details such as currently available instrument/facility for mentioned projects and/or discuss about your interest.

Short Term Project: Pulse Detector

This project involves designing a pulse detector with independently tunable lower and upper threshold set points. Pulses will be generated using a simple mechanical-to-electrical conversion method. The primary focus will be on designing modified Schmitt trigger circuits to meet design specifications, typically ranging from 0 to 5V and 1 to 10 microseconds time scale.

Detailed Description

In this project, you will develop a pulse detector system capable of independently adjusting lower and upper threshold set points. The pulses, generated through a mechanical-to-electrical conversion process, will be detected and analyzed using a modified Schmitt trigger circuit. This project aims to provide practical experience in circuit design and fine-tuning for precise electronic measurements.

Key Objectives
  • Design and Development: Create a pulse detector with independently tunable threshold set points using modified Schmitt trigger circuits.
  • Pulse Generation: Develop a simple mechanical-to-electrical conversion method to generate pulses.
  • Circuit Specification: Ensure the circuit meets design specifications, typically ranging from 0 to 5V and 1 to 10 microseconds time scale.
  • Testing and Validation: Test the pulse detector for accuracy and reliability in detecting pulses within the specified range.
Expected Skills and Learning Outcomes
  • Hands-on experience with designing and implementing Schmitt trigger circuits.
  • Understanding of pulse generation and detection techniques. Standard Master course solid-state lab experiment knowledge is expected.
  • Ability to adjust and tune electronic circuits for specific design requirements.
  • Skills in testing and validating electronic systems. Expected design is within the typical master course lab experiment type.
Short Term Project: 16-Channel Data Logger for STM Setup

In this project, you will develop a standalone data collection and plotting unit tailored for Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM). Utilizing simple electronics on a breadboard, you will gather and process data using a single-board computer or microcontroller. While not exactly an oscilloscope, the device will mimic its functionalities to meet our specific requirements.

Detailed Description

In this project, you will undertake the development of a 16-channel data logger designed specifically for use with a Scanning Tunneling Microscope (STM) setup. This data logger will serve as a standalone unit for collecting and plotting data, utilizing basic electronics assembled on a breadboard.

Key Objectives
  • Design and Development: Create a functional data logger capable of handling 16 channels using breadboard electronics.
  • Data Collection: Implement data collection mechanisms to gather accurate readings from the STM setup.
  • Data Processing: Process the collected data using a single-board computer or microcontroller.
  • Plotting: Develop a plotting feature to visualize the data in real-time.
Expected Skills and Learning Outcomes
  • Hands-on experience with electronics design and assembly on a breadboard.
  • Understanding of data logging and processing techniques.
  • Familiarity with single-board computers or microcontrollers for data handling.
  • Ability to visualize and plot data in real-time.
Short Term Project Micron range spacial resolution scanning AC Piezoelectric Response Analyzer

This project aims to develop a mesoscopic AC piezoelectric response analyzer from scratch using off-the-shelf components.

Detailed Description

In this project, you will be tasked with designing and building a mesoscopic AC piezoelectric response analyzer. The analyzer will be capable of measuring the piezoelectric response of materials at the mesoscopic scale, providing valuable insights into their electromechanical properties.

Key Objectives
  • Design and Development: Develop a complete AC piezoelectric response analyzer system using readily available components.
  • Circuit Design: Design the necessary circuits for generating AC signals and measuring the corresponding piezoelectric responses.
  • System Integration: Integrate all components into a cohesive system and ensure proper functionality.
  • Calibration and Testing: Calibrate the analyzer and conduct extensive testing to validate its accuracy and reliability.
Expected Skills and Learning Outcomes
  • Hands-on experience in circuit design and development for piezoelectric applications.
  • Understanding of mesoscopic-scale measurements and their significance in materials science.
  • Proficiency in system integration and calibration techniques.
  • Ability to analyze and interpret experimental data related to piezoelectric responses.

Photoemission facility

Equipment details

The instrument consists of both monochromatized and non-monochromatized X-ray light, and ultraviolet light sources. It also includes a hemispherical electron energy analyzer for photoemission spectroscopy studies.

For surface preparation, we have Ar gas sputtering, annealing (heating), and scraping facilities.

Characterization methods

  • X-ray photoemission spectroscopy
  • Ultraviolet photoemission spectroscopy
  • Inverse photoemission spectroscopy (design and actions are under plan/progress)
